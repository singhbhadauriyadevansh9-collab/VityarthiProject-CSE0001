# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study

## Topic: Phishing

---

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker disguises themselves as a trusted entity — such as a bank, university, or popular website — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or bank account details.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **The attacker crafts a fake message** — This is usually an email, SMS, or WhatsApp message designed to look exactly like it came from a legitimate source (e.g., "Your SBI account has been suspended. Click here to verify.").

2. **The victim receives the message** — The message creates a sense of urgency or fear (e.g., "Your account will be closed in 24 hours").

3. **The victim clicks the link** — The link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one (same logo, colours, layout).

4. **The victim enters their credentials** — Believing the site is real, the victim types in their username, password, or OTP.

5. **The attacker captures the data** — The entered details go directly to the attacker's server, not the real organization.

6. **Misuse of credentials** — The attacker uses the stolen details to access bank accounts, social media, or college portals — often within minutes.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- First-year college students unfamiliar with online security
- Senior citizens using digital banking for the first time
- Anyone who receives fake scholarship or job offer emails
- Users of UPI apps who receive fake refund or cashback messages

---

## Consequences

- Financial loss — bank accounts drained via UPI or net banking
- Identity theft — personal details used to open fraudulent accounts or take loans
- Academic harm — college login credentials stolen to access marks or submit fake documents
- Emotional distress — victims often feel shame, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital systems
