# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

1. ✅ **Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages.** Always go directly to the official website by typing the URL in your browser.

2. ✅ **Check the sender's email address carefully.** Fake emails often use addresses like "support@sbi-india-help.com" instead of the real "support@sbi.co.in".

3. ✅ **Look for HTTPS and a padlock icon** in your browser's address bar before entering any personal information on a website.

4. ✅ **Use a unique, strong password for every account.** Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager app if needed.

5. ✅ **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — Google, LinkedIn, GitHub, and banking apps.

6. ✅ **Keep your phone and apps updated.** Security updates patch vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

---

## UPI & Financial Safety (India-Specific)

7. ✅ **Never share your UPI PIN with anyone — not even bank officials.** Legitimate banks and apps will NEVER ask for your PIN.

8. ✅ **Be suspicious of unexpected "refund" or "cashback" requests.** Scammers pose as e-commerce customer service agents and ask you to scan a QR code — scanning a QR code to receive money is a scam. You only scan to send money.

9. ✅ **Verify UPI payment requests before approving** — check the merchant name and amount carefully on your UPI app screen.

10. ✅ **Do not install screen-sharing apps (like AnyDesk or TeamViewer) if asked by someone who called you.** This is a common banking scam.

---

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime:

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (24x7, toll-free)
- 📍 You can also visit your nearest Cyber Crime Police Station.

**Report immediately — the faster you report financial fraud, the higher the chance of recovery.**
