# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a real name on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub.

2. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?

3. **Use privacy settings** — Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal details on every platform.

4. **Fact-check before sharing** — Verify information from credible sources before forwarding news, statistics, or images.

5. **Engage respectfully** — Respond to others' posts and comments in a polite, constructive manner even when you disagree.

6. **Separate personal and professional accounts** — Consider maintaining a LinkedIn profile distinct from casual social accounts like Instagram or Twitter/X.

7. **Credit original creators** — When sharing someone else's content, always give credit to the original author or source.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your phone number, home address, daily schedule, or Aadhaar/PAN details on any platform.

2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — Never participate in online harassment. If you witness it, report it through the platform's tools.

3. **Don't post during exams or important events** — Excessive social media activity during academic periods reflects poorly and affects focus.

4. **Don't tag others without permission** — Always ask before tagging classmates or friends in photos or posts that could affect their reputation.

5. **Don't believe every message asking for money or urgency** — Scam messages ("Your account will be deleted unless you click here") are common. Verify through official channels.

6. **Don't use social media to complain about professors or the institution** — This can have academic and professional consequences.

7. **Don't share your location in real time** — Posting live check-ins or location tags can compromise your personal safety.
